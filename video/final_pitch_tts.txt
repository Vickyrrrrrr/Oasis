Climate change is irreversibly altering our environment. Across South Asia and vulnerable regions worldwide, extreme heatwaves are no longer anomalies; they are the new normal. But our primary defense against this crisis is failing. Traditional air conditioning is expensive, highly energy-intensive, and fundamentally dependent on stable electricity. When the grid fails under massive peak loads, millions of people are left without access to life-saving cooling systems. We are fighting a losing battle. 

Instead of treating cooling as an appliance problem, OASIS reimagines it as an infrastructure and housing design challenge. OASIS is a modular, passive-first cooling shelter. It is engineered from the ground up to drastically reduce our dependency on conventional HVAC systems and fragile power grids.

The foundation of OASIS is its advanced thermal battery. By integrating Phase Change Materials directly into the structural walls, the system passively absorbs excess environmental heat during the day. This locks the internal core temperature at a stable 28 degrees Celsius, without using a single watt of electricity. But sensible heat—the temperature you read on a thermometer—is only half the battle. In regions experiencing the monsoon crisis, high humidity prevents sweat from evaporating, causing deadly heat index spikes even at moderate temperatures. 

To combat humidity, OASIS relies on autonomous intelligence. At the heart of the shelter is a customized five-dollar TinyML chip. This autonomous Edge AI continuously monitors environmental metrics. When humidity spikes, the AI activates high-efficiency desiccant scrubbing and automated night-flushing systems. It provides active, intelligent latent cooling exactly when it is needed, optimizing energy use down to the millisecond.

The impact is staggering. While traditional HVAC systems demand immense continuous power, the entire OASIS ecosystem operates on less than eight hundred watt-hours per day. It is so efficient that it can be entirely sustained by a single, standard solar panel. This means true off-grid resilience. It means democratized cooling for urban slums, remote villages, and disaster relief zones. 

OASIS began as a compact emergency cooling shelter. But its implications are far greater. It is a paradigm shift in how we build. OASIS is not merely a cooling shelter. It is a working prototype for how future housing systems must adapt sustainably to a warming planet. This is the future of resilient infrastructure. This is OASIS.
