# OASIS: Pitch & Introduction Script
*(Target length: 2 to 3 minutes | Approx. 350 words)*

---

**(Slide 1: Title & The Problem - 30 seconds)**
"Hello everyone. When we talk about global warming, our first instinct is to build better, more efficient Air Conditioners. But treating extreme heat as an 'appliance problem' is a losing battle. 

In heatwave-prone urban regions, disaster relief camps, and rural low-electricity areas, the grid simply cannot support the massive power demands of traditional HVAC systems. For climate-vulnerable communities, air conditioning isn't just expensive—it's inaccessible.

That’s why we created **OASIS**."

**(Slide 2: The Core Philosophy & Solution - 45 seconds)**
"OASIS isn't an AC replacement. It is a **modular, passive cooling infrastructure prototype** that reduces our dependency on HVAC systems entirely. 

Instead of fighting the heat with compressors, we use the environment to our advantage through passive-first thermal engineering. By integrating specific Phase Change Materials—like Glauber’s Salt and CrodaTherm 29—into the high-solar-load surfaces of the structure, the walls themselves become a 'thermal battery.' During a 45-degree day, the walls passively absorb massive amounts of heat, locking the internal temperature at a safe 28 degrees without using a single drop of electricity."

**(Slide 3: The Edge AI & Humidity Breakthrough - 45 seconds)**
"But sensible cooling isn't enough. Our critical engineering discovery during development was that during monsoons, high humidity causes the 'Heat Index' to spike to deadly levels, even if the room is 28 degrees. 

To solve this, we integrated an autonomous Edge AI system powered by a $5 ESP32 chip and TinyML. When it detects dangerous humidity spikes, the AI engages a low-power desiccant-assisted airflow system to actively scrub moisture from the air. At night, it opens servo-controlled vents and blasts cool night air into the walls to 're-freeze' the thermal battery for the next day."

**(Slide 4: Scalability & Vision - 30 seconds)**
"Because we optimized our Phase Change Material placement, we reduced material costs by nearly 50%. The entire active system consumes less than 800 watt-hours a day, running easily on a single solar panel. 

OASIS is not merely a cooling shelter. From temporary relief camps to modular room-scale systems, it is a prototype for how future housing systems may adapt sustainably to a warming planet. 

Thank you."
